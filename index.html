About the Club

Our Story

From humble community roots to the National First Division — the Casric Stars FC journey is one of ambition, grit, and collective pride.

Casric Stars FC is a South African professional football club based in KwaMhlanga, Mpumalanga. Founded in 2012/13 as FC Palmeiras, the club initially competed in the North West stream of the ABC Motsepe League — the third tier of South African football.

During the 2017/18 season, the club rebranded to Casric FC, marking a new chapter of ambition. The club steadily built its reputation in the regional football scene, developing local talent and building a strong bond with the KwaMhlanga community.

The defining moment arrived in August 2022, when Casric purchased the National First Division status from Free State Stars for R12 million. Rebranded as Casric Stars FC and relocated to Mpumalanga, the club debuted in the NFD for the 2022/23 season.

Today, Casric Stars FC competes under the Premier Soccer League with one clear ambition: promotion to the DStv Premiership.

About the Ground

Casric Stars FC plays its home matches in KwaMhlanga, Mpumalanga. The ground serves as a focal point for the local community, bringing professional National First Division football to the province for the first time following the club's historic 2022 PSL status acquisition.


On matchdays the ground comes alive with the colours of gold and cyan as local supporters rally behind the Stars in their pursuit of promotion to the DStv Premiership.

Getting There

  • KwaMhlanga is in Nkangala District, Mpumalanga — approximately 70km northeast of Pretoria.
  • By road: Take the N4 east from Pretoria, exit at Bronkhorstspruit, follow R25 north toward KwaMhlanga.
  • Public transport connections available from Pretoria and eMalahleni (Witbank) taxi routes.
  • Parking available at and around the ground on matchdays.
  • Follow club channels for matchday updates, kick-off times, and ticketing.
